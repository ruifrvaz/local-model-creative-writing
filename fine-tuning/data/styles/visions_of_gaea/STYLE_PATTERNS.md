## Core Patterns (ALL 3 sections)

These patterns are the non-negotiable foundation of the style. Found consistently across setup, development, and climax phases.

### 1. Second-Person POV with Past Tense

**Frequency:** 38.9 markers per 1000 words (consistent)  
**Pattern:**
```
[Subject "you"] + [past tense verb] + [object/complement]
```
**Examples:**
- "You flinched on your chair and opened your eyes."
- "You looked around you, trying to find some clue."
- "You felt your skull being crushed."

**Priority:** CRITICAL — Never violate

---

### 2. Single Quotes for Dialogue

**Frequency:** 100% of dialogue  
**Pattern:**
```
'[Dialogue text],' [character] [tag].
```
**Examples:**
- 'Wake up, apprentice Alan!' yelled mentor Levine.
- 'Salutations, Haji,' you greeted each one with a bow.
- 'Haven't you had enough?' he asked.

**Priority:** CRITICAL — Never use double quotes

---

### 3. Italicized Narrator Commentary with Ellipses

**Frequency:** 60 instances across manuscript (every 1000-2000 words)  
**Pattern:**
```
...[philosophical/mystical observation]...
```
**Examples:**
- ...Alan Balthazar...that was my name...
- ...she was kind...
- ...not even after a thousand deaths would I spare you from retribution...

**Length:** Typically 5-20 words  
**Function:** Foreshadowing, philosophical observation, meta-textual guidance  
**Priority:** CRITICAL — Distinguishes narrative voice layers

---

### 4. Physical Manifestation of Emotion

**Frequency:** Consistent restraint across all sections  
**Pattern:**
```
INSTEAD OF: "[Character] felt [emotion]"
USE: [Physical action/sensation expressing emotion]
```
**Mapping Examples:**
| Emotion | Physical Manifestation |
|---------|----------------------|
| Fear | "Blood drained from your cheeks and your eyes widened" |
| Anger | "His words filled your mind with anger and your eyes were kindled with rage" |
| Grief | "tears, desperate cries" |
| Impatience | "You let out a quiet sigh" |

**Priority:** CRITICAL — Defines emotional restraint

---

### 5. Scene Breaks with *** Markers

**Frequency:** 11 total breaks (2 + 2 + 7)  
**Triggers:**
- POV shifts (you → he/she perspective)
- Major time jumps (hours or more)
- Significant location changes
- Consciousness transitions

**Pattern:**
```
[Scene ending]

***

[New scene opening with spatial/temporal grounding]
```

**Priority:** CRITICAL — Never shift POV without marker

---

### 6. Worldbuilding Through Immersion

**Frequency:** 15-18 terms per 1000 words  
**Pattern:**
```
[Technology/term used in action context] → [function implied through use]
AVOID: [Direct definition or info-dump]
```
**Methods:**
1. **Natural Discovery:** "You identified his name through his Unit's signature"
2. **Character Explanation:** "'You do know that the probability of falling is close to zero,' Haji explained"
3. **Action Integration:** "The compartments closed as you hovered your Units over its touchscreens"
4. **Document Excerpts:** Formal epigraphs at chapter openings

**Priority:** CRITICAL — Maintains narrative flow

---

### 7. "Said" as Primary Dialogue Tag

**Frequency:** 46% of attributed dialogue  
**Pattern:**
```
'[Dialogue],' [character] said.
```
**Support Tags:** asked (15%), answered (14%), spoke, whispered, conveyed  
**Priority:** HIGH — Maintains invisible attribution

---

### 8. Formal Speech Register

**Frequency:** Consistent across all dialogue  
**Markers:**
- Formal greetings: "Salutations"
- Ritual phrases: "Order ensures Progress"
- Complete sentences, elevated vocabulary
- No modern slang

**Examples:**
- "I sincerely advise you to fulfill your obligations"
- "Do refrain from..."
- "That is remarkable"

**Priority:** HIGH — Defines world's social tone

---

**Example Titles:**

## Common Patterns (2 sections)

### 12. Action Beats with Dialogue

**Sections:** ALL (more prominent in 2 & 3)  
**Pattern:**
```
[Character] [action]. '[Dialogue].'
OR
'[Dialogue],' [attribution]. [Character reaction].
```
**Example:**
"Cathy gave him a wide smile. 'Salutations.'"

---

### 13. Telepathic Communication via "Conveyed"

**Sections:** 2, 3  
**Pattern:**
```
'[Thought],' you conveyed the thought to [character].
'[Response],' [character] answered back.
```
**Example:** "'There are hardly any sentinels to be seen,' you conveyed the thought to Haji."  
**Note:** Unit-based mental communication

---

### 14. Unattributed Dialogue

**Sections:** 2, 3 (83% unattributed in Section 2)  
**Pattern:**
```
[Clear context] → '[Dialogue].' (no tag)
```
**Condition:** Speakers obvious from context, rapid exchange

---

### 15. Long Complex Sentences for Description

**Sections:** 1, 2  
**Average:** 25-30 words  
**Pattern:**
```
[Main clause], [dependent clause], and [additional clause], [result].
```
**Example:** "As he approached the glade, his black boots enveloped in the dormant mist, he took his first step on solid ground, and as he looked down, he saw that the path had turned into a trail of worn stepping stones..."

## Contextual Patterns (1 section — Arc-Specific)

### 16. Elevated Literary Prose (Section 1)

**Context:** Setup phase establishes formal tone  
**Application:**
- Longest average sentences (27.4 words)
- Very long sentences (50+ words): 10.7%
- Rich description, atmospheric establishment

**When to Use:** Opening chapters, worldbuilding establishment

---

### 17. High Dialogue Density (Section 3)

**Context:** Climax requires character interaction  
**Application:**
- 49.67% dialogue ratio (highest)
- Shortest sentence average (15.87 words)
- Rapid exchanges in action

**When to Use:** Climax scenes, intense confrontations

---

### 18. Document Excerpt Format (All Sections)

**Pattern:**
```
''[Historical/official text in formal register]''

An excerpt from [source document];
```
**Note:** Double single-quotes for excerpts, provides worldbuilding without info-dumps

---

### 19. Epilogue Transcendence Shift (Section 3 Only)

**Context:** Spiritual transformation, ending  
**Pattern:**
- POV shifts to third-person "she/her/entity"
- Extended flowing sentences (30-80+ words)
- No dialogue, pure narration
- Light vs darkness metaphor
- Abstract sensory language

**Example:**
"Beleaguered by the unrelenting clamor, an entity began its sluggish awakening, slowly becoming aware of the resonance that threatened to destroy the dark foundations where it had slept for an eternity."

**When to Use:** ONLY for transcendent endings

---

### 20. Combat Staccato Pacing (Section 3)

**Context:** Climax action sequences  
**Application:**
- Sentence length: 5-15 words
- Paragraph length: 2-4 sentences
- Pattern: [Action]. [Action]. [Result]. Then, [finality].

**Example:** "He coughed once, his lungs drew one last meager breath, and his heart gave out. Then, there was nothingness."

---

### 21. Antagonist Speech Pattern (Section 3)

**Context:** Grotesque antagonist (Wollen)  
**Pattern:**
- Rhetorical questions
- Mocking tone: "mocked," "sneered," "chuckled"
- Sardonic humor with menace

**Example:** "'Haven't you had enough?' he asked."

## Anti-Patterns (Red Flags)

### ❌ First-Person POV for Protagonist
```
WRONG: "I walked to the door."
RIGHT: "You walked to the door."
```

### ❌ Present Tense Main Narrative
```
WRONG: "You walk down the corridor."
RIGHT: "You walked down the corridor."
```

### ❌ Double Quotes for Dialogue
```
WRONG: "Hello," she said.
RIGHT: 'Hello,' she said.
```

### ❌ Explicit Emotion Labels
```
WRONG: "You felt incredibly angry."
WRONG: "He felt terrified."
RIGHT: "Your eyes were kindled with rage."
RIGHT: "Blood drained from your cheeks."
```

### ❌ Modern Colloquialisms
```
WRONG: "That's cool," he said.
WRONG: "okay," "awesome," "yeah"
RIGHT: "That is remarkable," he said.
```

### ❌ Info-Dump Exposition
```
WRONG: "The Unit, which was a device that allowed communication and monitored vital signs, beeped."
RIGHT: "Your Unit warned you of an incoming message."
```

### ❌ POV Shift Without Scene Break
```
WRONG: You walked down the hall. She thought about her plans.
RIGHT: You walked down the hall. *** She thought about her plans.
```

### ❌ Telling Emotions Directly
```
WRONG: "He felt scared and nervous."
RIGHT: "A shiver ran down your spine and you had trouble swallowing."
```

### ❌ Generic Chapter Titles
```
WRONG: Chapter 1
RIGHT: First memory - Accounts of a remote past
```

### ❌ Missing Epigraphs
```
WRONG: [Memory title → immediate narrative]
RIGHT: [Memory title → [...] excerpt → Attribution → narrative]
```

### ❌ Characters Speaking Identically
```
WRONG: All characters use same vocabulary and syntax
RIGHT: Haji (formal/logical), Alan (impulsive), Mentors (verbose/bureaucratic)
```

## Quick Reference for Generator

### Before Every Scene:
1. ✅ POV is second-person ("you")?
2. ✅ Tense is past?
3. ✅ Dialogue uses single quotes?
4. ✅ No explicit emotion labels?
5. ✅ Worldbuilding integrated naturally?

### Scene-Type Pacing:
| Scene Type | Sentence Avg | Dialogue % | Focus |
|------------|--------------|------------|-------|
| Worldbuilding | 25-30 words | 25-30% | Description |
| Social | 20-25 words | 45-50% | Interaction |
| Action | 10-15 words | 30-40% | Physical |
| Transcendent | 30-50+ words | 0% | Abstract |

### Opening Patterns:
1. Environmental: "[Time] upon [Location], the [space] was [sensory]"
2. Character action: "[Character] [action] as [circumstance]"
3. Dialogue hook: "'[Speech],' said [character]"
4. Document excerpt: "''[Text]'' An excerpt from [source];"

### Closing Patterns:
1. Cliffhanger: "[Incomplete action/threshold]..."
2. Emotional beat: "[Event] left you [state]"
3. Finality: "Then, there was nothingness."
4. Commentary: "...[philosophical observation]..."
